How to Sprout

Sprouts are germinated seeds that are harvested before they can grow roots and leaves. Select a variety from the dropdown list below.

Notes

These mild sprouts are similar in taste and appearance to alfalfa sprouts. They expand a lot when sprouting, so be sure to use a large sprouting jar.

Instructions

  1. Fill clean sprouting jar with cold water and soak

    clover seeds

    for

    6 to 10 hours

  2. Invert jar and shake to drain, then rest jar at slight angle in bowl or sink to allow excess water to drain
  3. Keep jar out of direct sunlight
  4. Rinse, invert, drain, and rest jar at slight angle at least twice a day for

    4 to 6 days

  5. Enjoy sprouts as soon as “tails” emerge or continue sprouting until desired size or taste is reached
  6. Sprouts can be stored in paper towel-lined container in refrigerator for up to a week

Suggested uses

  • Salads and sandwiches
  • Stir fries
  • Soups, slaws, and more
